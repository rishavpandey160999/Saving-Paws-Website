What we do



Saving Paws is a new initiative started to feed and provide medical relief to all the street animals. It is run by a group of volunteers from Ranchi and we rescue hundreds of animals every year. We use their stories and our work to inspire and engage both the local community and people around the world.

We rescue street animals, provide medical care and sanctuary, and help turn community compassion into action. We combine on-the-ground, immediate relief to suffering animals with prevention to ensure long-term protection for animals.

Useful To Know

Cats
Cats are believed to be the only mammals who don't taste sweetness. Cats are nearsighted, but their peripheral vision and night vision are much better than that of humans. Cats are supposed to have 18 toes (five toes on each front paw; four toes on each back paw). Cats can jump up to six times their length.

Rodents
Rodents are mammals of the order Rodentia, which are characterized by a single pair of continuously growing incisors in each of the upper and lower jaws. About 40% of all mammal species are rodents. They are extremely diverse in their ecology and lifestyles and can be found in almost every terrestrial habitats, including human-made environments.
Birds
Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeleton. Birds live worldwide and range in size from the 5.5 cm (2.2 in) bee hummingbird to the 2.8 m (9 ft 2 in) ostrich.
Fish
Fish are aquatic, craniate, gill-bearing animals that lack limbs with digits. They form a sister group to the tunicates, together forming the olfactores. The earliest organisms that can be classified as fish were soft-bodied chordates that first appeared during the Cambrian period.
Reptiles
Reptiles are tetrapod animals in the class or clade Reptilia. As a class in Linnean taxonomy, Reptilia refers to a paraphyletic grouping comprising all amniotes except synapsids and Aves. The class Reptilia comprises turtles, crocodilians, snakes, amphisbaenians, lizards, tuatara, and their extinct relatives.
